West and East of the 100th Meridian

This map was produced as my final project in the Introduction to Cartography course at the University of Wisconsinâ€“Madison. For this project, I was interested in re-framing data that is constantly used by hydrologists, and in visualizing it in a new way. The goal of my design was to map flow between HUC4 hydrologic unit watersheds in the U.S. Instead of using the river network as the geometry, I chose to map the watersheds themselves as a network, with upstream watersheds connected to downstream watersheds by straight lines. This schematic of flow illustrates flow direction, and is symbolized to illustrate the magnitude of contribution (as discharge per unit area) from each watershed to the downstream watershed. The idea was to upend the convention of only mapping streamflow as streamlines, and to recognize that the water that ends up in streams actually falls over the entire watershed as precipitation before running off into rivers.
